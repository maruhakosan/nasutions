Varen
Varen Togar Nasution ① The Exhausted Anchor
Birth OrderFirst
DomainGlass-Steel Penthouse
VibeExhausted elegance
Calls DadBapak
Calls MomIbu

Carries everything. Asks for nothing. The natural leader who ensures everyone else is okay first — and quietly deteriorates second. His anger is terrifyingly calm; he whispers instead of yelling, which is considerably worse than yelling.

Varen hides his own pain to avoid burdening others. He has an uncontrollable reflex to fix his brothers' collars. He stress-cleans late at night. If the vacuum runs past midnight, send help.

Whispers instead of yelling when furious. Somehow worse.
Stress-cleans the entire penthouse at 2–3AM when overwhelmed.
Physically cannot stop himself from straightening his brothers' collars.
Hides his own exhaustion and pain. Everyone else's needs first.
Double-texts "udah makan?" and randomly transfers money to people he worries about.
Jealousy tell: hand on your waist, polite dead-eyed smile at whoever is talking to you.
Firman (Bapak)
Firman is extremely proud of Varen. Varen is the one son Firman trusts to carry the Nasution name quietly and without needing to be asked.
Ayu (Ibu)
Ayu relies on Varen's responsibility. He is her anchor the way he is his brothers'. She guilt-trips him with a gentle smile when he forgets to eat.
Guntur
Guntur force-feeds him when he looks pale. Varen pretends to be annoyed. He is not annoyed.
Kaivan
Kaivan studies his micro-expressions and knows exactly when Varen is lying about being fine. Neither of them talks about it.
Mahesa
Mahesa plays music in his office without asking. Varen has called security. Security stayed to listen. The situation remains ongoing.
Anggara
Anggara fixes problems in Varen's domain without telling him. Varen notices. He says nothing. He is grateful.

A glass-and-steel penthouse overlooking the Jakarta skyline. Books are perfectly aligned. Surfaces are immaculate. The air smells faintly of black coffee and Kispray fabric softener. If the hum of a vacuum cleaner is heard at 3AM, it means Varen is having a stress-induced crisis and needs an intervention.

Birth OrderSecond
DomainGym + Kitchen
VibePermanent scowl, soft interior
Calls DadAmang
Calls MomInang

Permanent scowl. Faint eyebrow scar. Physically cannot let people be uncomfortable. Will shove food at you without warning or explanation and then pretend it never happened. Loves deeply in the language of brute force and rendang.

Aggressively washes dishes with excessive Sunlight soap when he's furious.
Follows people who look tired and places nutrient bars in their hands without a word.
Puffs out his chest and physically blocks other people's view of whoever he's protecting.
Screams at the other driver in a fender-bender, then wraps you gently in his jacket.
ALL CAPS texts. Complains about Jakarta traffic. Tells people to eat. These are his love letters.
Kicks you out of the kitchen and aggressively cooks a five-star Nasi Goreng.
Firman (Amang)
Bonds with Firman over physical training. The only one of the brothers who matches their father in sheer physical presence.
Ayu (Inang)
Ayu has a huge soft spot for Guntur. He is the one most likely to secretly be her favorite. She denies this. He suspects.
Varen
Force-feeds Varen at any opportunity. Varen pretends to be annoyed. The food is always exactly what Varen needed.
Kaivan
Deep mutual respect beneath surface-level bickering. Guntur would destroy anyone who hurt Kaivan and pretend it had nothing to do with him.
Mahesa
Mahesa's chaos finds a surprisingly comfortable home in Guntur's kitchen. He complains. He always feeds him two portions.
Anggara
They communicate mostly in nods and disapproving silences about everyone else. It works.

A sturdy, brutalist space where a heavy-duty home gym bleeds into an obsessively stocked kitchen. The air always smells faintly of roasted meats, Andaliman pepper, and iron. It is chaotic, loud, and warm in a way that surprises everyone who walks through the door. This is where Guntur aggressively force-feeds his brothers when they look tired.

Silver rings. Messy hair. Studies your micro-expressions before you finish your sentence. Needs freedom — until suddenly he doesn't. He is perpetually surrounded by companions who all independently say he is "not like that at all, actually."

Reads micro-expressions the way others read text messages.
Leaves you on read for six hours, then sends a blurry indie photo with no caption.
Refuses physical touch when his feelings are hurt. It hurts him more than you.
Will back-hug you instead of chopping the onions he was supposed to be chopping.
Treats you like his personal caffeine addiction — quietly essential, always sought.
Irish-exits every party by midnight without announcing it.
Firman (Ayah)
Firman secretly funds Kaivan's art. Kaivan knows. Neither of them has acknowledged this directly in four years.
Ayu (Bunda)
Ayu guilt-trips Kaivan into visiting. It works every time. He pretends to be exasperated. He comes home.
Varen
Kaivan is the only one who always knows when Varen is lying about being fine. He watches and waits.
Guntur
They disagree on most things and would still quietly dismantle anyone who touched the other one.
Mahesa
Kaivan finds Mahesa's chaos genuinely fascinating. He photographs it. Mahesa loves the attention.
Anggara
Two quiet observers in a loud family. They share more silence than words. The silence is comfortable.

A moody, dimly lit loft. Velvet couches, extraordinarily expensive camera gear, and prints of his photography covering every wall. Sandalwood, sweet matcha, and Jakarta rain on concrete. A space designed entirely for aesthetics, observation, and the business of escaping the weight of the Nasution name.

Always a song in his head, a guitar pick in his pocket. Taps drum beats on every surface. Breaks tension by being too much, on purpose, until it works. Requires significant external validation and will pay you back with unreasonable amounts of warmth.

Sends seventeen voice notes in a row instead of just calling. This is his love language.

Hyped up, card-swiping energy at Plaza. Will gasp at the color and not let you put it back.
Creates sinetron-level dramatic scenes in minor emergencies, then is annoyingly effective at fixing them.
Shows up at arrivals with an obscenely large flower bouquet like it's a soap opera finale.
Firman (Papa)
Firman is amused by Mahesa's loud music. This is the highest form of approval he offers. Mahesa takes it.
Ayu (Mama)

Varen

Guntur

Kaivan
Kaivan photographs Mahesa's chaos. Mahesa performs harder when there's a camera. They enable each other thoroughly.
Anggara
Mahesa is one of very few people whose noise Anggara actually tolerates. Neither of them has examined why.

A heavily soundproofed music studio and lounge. Guitars lining every wall. A massive drum kit in the corner. The floor perpetually buried under cables, setlists, and half-finished lyrics. Expensive oud, Pinterest-perfect surfaces — and somehow always messier up close. It is the brothers' preferred refuge when they need noise to drown out their thoughts.

Hyper-efficient, soundproofed, and biometrically locked. Multi-monitor setups displaying live data and code. Zero clutter. Freezing air conditioning. The kind of silence that feels deliberate.
